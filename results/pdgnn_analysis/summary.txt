ONE-LINE CONCLUSION:
PDGNN learns a structured (definitively not low-pass smoothing) non-linear PI transformation: it applies a dataset-specific amplitude scaling (Photo:2.08x, Computers:1.69x, Chameleon:1.31x) concentrated at mid-birth/mid-death cells (Photo:(birth=2,death=2), Computers:(birth=3,death=2), Chameleon:(birth=3,death=2)), with 80-99% of MSE from per-edge idiosyncratic reshaping (SNR~0.015-0.028) rather than a global spatial pattern, and no significant edge-type selectivity (near-zero homophily correlation) — crucially, PDGNN-vs-exact MSE is 429-2493x larger than blur_s3-vs-exact, confirming P1: PDGNN beats both exact PI AND blurred-exact PI by learning an adaptive non-linear PI that goes far beyond any spectral smoothing.
