INTERPRETATION:
- MUTAG: PI signal lives overwhelmingly in H1 (rings). SVM on H1
  alone = 82.0% vs H0 alone = 66.0%. The H1-only PI is, by itself,
  as accurate as the full withPI GIN. This confirms the hypothesis:
  for MUTAG the PI benefit comes from H1 (rings/loops).
- PROTEINS: H0 and H1 carry near-identical, weak signal (68.0 vs
  68.7%); 'both' is marginally best (69.5%). No single dimension
  dominates, and in GIN the PI gives no gain at all (within noise).
- GIN vs SVM divergence on MUTAG (GIN says H0, SVM says H1): GIN
  already learns ring/degree structure from message passing, so the
  H1 PI is largely redundant to it; the small residual H0 PI signal
  (degree/component statistics) is what GIN can still exploit on top.
  All GIN per-variant gaps (+2.07 to +3.16%p) sit well within one
  std (~6-7%p), so the GIN driver call is not statistically robust.
  The PI's intrinsic class signal (SVM) is clearly H1 for MUTAG.

CONCLUSION: For molecules where PI genuinely helps (MUTAG), the
class signal is carried by H1 (rings/loops), confirmed by SVM on PI
features alone. For PROTEINS the PI carries little usable signal in
either dimension and gives no GIN gain.
